Introduction

Ownership structure refers to the way a business is owned and controlled. It determines who makes decisions, who earns profits, and how responsibilities are shared. Different ownership structures suit different types of businesses.

Sole Proprietorship

    Ownership structure refers to the way a business is owned and controlled. It determines who makes decisions, who earns profits, and how responsibilities are shared. Different ownership structures suit different types of businesses.

Joint Hindu Family Business

    A Joint Hindu Family Business is owned by members of a Hindu family and managed by the eldest member, known as the Karta. It follows traditional practices and is based on family ownership. All members share profits, but the Karta takes major decisions.

Partnership

    A partnership is a business owned by two or more people who agree to share profits and losses. It is formed through a partnership agreement. Partners contribute capital, skills, and share responsibilities in running the business.

Co-operative Society

    A co-operative society is formed by a group of people to meet common needs. It is based on the principle of equality, where each member has equal rights. Its main aim is service rather than profit.

Joint Stock Company

    A joint stock company is a large business owned by shareholders. It has a separate legal identity from its owners. It can raise large capital and is suitable for large-scale operations.

Comparison

    Different ownership structures vary in size, control, and purpose. Sole proprietorship is simple, partnership involves shared responsibility, and companies are large and complex. Each structure is suitable for different business needs.

Conclusion

Ownership structures play a vital role in business success. Choosing the right structure helps in efficient management and growth. Each type has its own advantages depending on the situation.
